Grandma's No Bake Cookies

A Taste of What's To Come

This specific recipe for no-bake cookies comes from my Grandma and is one of my favorite treats from childhood. The basic recipe is super easy and quick to make, the main ingrediants being: cocoa, peanut butter and oats. Only a few minutes are required to prepare. Below the main recipe will be some suggestions from Mom on how to make them low-carb.

Ingrediants

  • clock with a second hand or the timer on your phone
  • wax paper, 2 sheets, about 18 inches long each
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 3 T.(tablespoons) cocoa
  • 1/4 lb. (1 stick) butter
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 cup peanut butter
  • 1 cup nuts, any kind, omitted if using crunchy peanut butter
  • 1 t.(teaspoon) vanilla
  • 3 cup oats

Directions

  1. Prep all ingredients before beginning. Timing is important.
  2. Mix sugar and cocoa. Set aside.
  3. Put peanut butter, nuts, vanilla, and oats within easy reach of the stove, if possible.
  4. Melt butter. Add milk. Add sugar and cocoa mixture. Heat on medium heat. When bubbles begin to form, start timer for 2 minutes. *Note from Mom(Recipe calls for continuous stirring, but that is marked out. But I think it will burn if I don't.) Remove from heat.
  5. Work quickly. Add peanut butter, nuts and vanilla. Drop by teaspoon onto wax paper, pressing to flatten. Let cool.
